\subsection*{3) What happens if you have too few items in a table row?}

\noindent If a row provides fewer cells than the preamble declares (here \(3\) columns),
the missing cells are left empty.

\medskip
\begin{tabular}{lll}
Animal & Food & Size \\
dog    & meat & medium \\
horse  & hay  \\        % missing the 3rd cell -> empty cell
frog   & flies& small \\
\end{tabular}

\bigskip

\subsection*{4) What happens if you have too many items in a table row?}

\noindent If a row provides more cells than declared, LaTeX produces an error (commonly
\emph{``Extra alignment tab has been changed to \textbackslash cr''}). The following code
would \textbf{not} compile if used as-is:

\medskip
\begin{verbatim}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
Animal & Food & Size \\
dog    & meat & medium & EXTRA \\ % too many cells (4 instead of 3)
horse  & hay  & large \\
\end{tabular}
\end{verbatim}

\noindent To fix it, either remove the extra cell, or declare \(4\) columns in the preamble.

\subsubsection*{5.2) Override formatting in a single cell using \texttt{\textbackslash multicolumn\{1\}\{...\}\{...\}}}

\noindent Here the first column is italic and adds a colon after each entry, but the header
cell is kept normal by using \texttt{\textbackslash multicolumn}.

\medskip
\begin{tabular}{>{\itshape}l<{:}ll}
\toprule
\multicolumn{1}{l}{Animal} & Food & Size \\
\midrule
dog   & meat  & medium \\
horse & hay   & large \\
frog  & flies & small \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
